Chapter 03

One campaign taught me how strategy becomes story.

Featured case / IPP Sachi Group

Repositioning a snack brand for younger audiences without losing its identity

The challenge was not simply to "make content younger." It was to shift audience attention away from Gen X and Gen Y habits and help the product feel more relevant to Millennials and Gen Z.

That meant looking closely at the gap between current performance and audience expectation, then creating a content direction that felt more native to younger viewers in both tone and presentation.

The brief

Reframe a new rice paper snack product so it feels more engaging for a younger audience.

The move

Audited current social media performance and mapped where the existing style was not connecting with younger users.

The result

Reached 5,500+ impressions on Facebook and gained view traction on TikTok during the repositioning effort.

The lesson

Algorithms may slow down at first when content style changes, but consistency and audience alignment improve momentum over time.
